Case #2024-002: Ransomware Analysis

Date of Investigation: May 02, 2024

Status: Closed

1. Executive Summary

On May 02, 2024, our incident response team was activated to investigate a ransomware infection affecting three critical domain controllers. The investigation revealed the threat actors gained initial access through an unpatched VPN vulnerability, escalated privileges, and deployed the LockBit 3.0 ransomware variant.

2. Initial Access & Lateral Movement

Forensic analysis of the external firewall logs indicated exploitation of CVE-2023-46805 on the corporate VPN appliance. Following initial access, the attackers utilized compromised credential dumps to move laterally via RDP (Remote Desktop Protocol) to internal servers, leaving Event ID 4624 (Logon) artifacts.

3. Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

  • SHA-256: 8a4c0... (Ransomware Executable)
  • IP Address: 193.168.0.X (Command & Control Server)
  • File Path: C:\Windows\Temp\svc_update.exe
  • Registry Key: HKLM\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run\UpdateService

4. Eradication & Remediation

The affected VPN appliance was taken offline and patched. The compromised domain controllers were rebuilt from clean backups taken prior to the initial access date. A firm-wide password reset was enforced for all domain accounts, and multi-factor authentication (MFA) was mandated for all external access points.
