Scenario: Incident Response

Ransomware Incident Response

Industry: Healthcare Provider

The Challenge

A mid-sized healthcare provider suffered a devastating ransomware attack at 2:00 AM on a Sunday. Critical patient databases were encrypted, bringing hospital operations to a halt. The client needed immediate containment, identification of the breach source (patient zero), and a safe path to recovery without paying the ransom.

Objectives

  • Isolate the infection and prevent further lateral movement.
  • Identify the initial access vector to patch the vulnerability.
  • Reverse-engineer the deployment mechanism to find IOCs.
  • Ensure backups were uncompromised before initiating recovery.

The Investigation Process

We deployed our rapid-response agents to the client's remaining servers to collect live memory dumps and triage data. The forensic timeline quickly narrowed down the attack origin to a legacy remote-access server.

Key Technical Discoveries:
  • Initial Access: Discovered an exposed RDP (Remote Desktop Protocol) port facing the public internet. Event logs (ID 4624/4625) showed weeks of brute-force attempts culminating in a successful login using a compromised vendor credential.
  • Lateral Movement: The attackers used `PsExec` to move from the legacy server to the main domain controller.
  • Payload Delivery: The ransomware (identified as a LockBit variant) was deployed network-wide via a malicious Group Policy Object (GPO) scheduled task.

The Outcome

Within 12 hours, we fully contained the breach by severing the compromised RDP connection and disabling the malicious GPO. We verified that the offline backup servers remained untouched by the encryption routine. The hospital successfully restored their patient databases from the clean backups, completely avoiding the ransom demand. We then implemented a firm-wide MFA mandate and audited all external-facing assets to prevent recurrence.
