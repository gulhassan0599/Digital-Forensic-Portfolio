Chain of Custody Explained

Aug 12, 2024 4 min read

In digital forensics, finding the smoking gun isn't enough. You have to prove that the smoking gun wasn't planted, altered, or mishandled by investigators. This is achieved through the Chain of Custody.

What is the Chain of Custody?

The Chain of Custody is a chronological, paper trail documenting the seizure, custody, control, transfer, analysis, and disposition of physical and electronic evidence.

Why is it Critical?

If digital evidence is presented in a court of law, opposing counsel will immediately attack how the evidence was handled. If there is a gap in the timeline where the whereabouts of a hard drive cannot be accounted for, the judge may rule the evidence inadmissible. A solid chain of custody guarantees authenticity.

Elements of a Good Chain of Custody Form

  • Item Description: Make, model, serial numbers, and physical condition.
  • Date and Time: Exact timestamps for every transfer of possession.
  • Signatures: Both the person releasing the evidence and the person receiving it must sign and print their names.
  • Purpose of Transfer: Why the evidence was moved (e.g., "Transferred to lab for imaging", "Returned to evidence locker").

Maintaining the chain of custody is a foundational principle of digital forensics. It bridges the gap between technical investigation and legal accountability.
